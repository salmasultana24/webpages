Lesson Plan: Story Telling

Lesson Title:

Mastering the Art of Storytelling

Target Group:

First-Year Undergraduate ESL Students

Duration:

90 minutes

Lesson Objectives:

  • Students will understand the structure and elements of effective storytelling.
  • Students will learn and practice narrative techniques and vocabulary.
  • Students will develop confidence in creating and delivering their own stories.

Lesson Outline:

Warm-Up Activity (10 minutes)

Activity: Story Sharing Circle

Instructions: Students sit in a circle. Each student shares a brief personal story (1-2 minutes) about a memorable event or experience.

Purpose: To introduce students to storytelling and create a comfortable engaging environment.

Introduction to Storytelling (15 minutes)

Content:

  • Definition: Explain that storytelling is the act of conveying events through words, sounds, and images, often by improvisation or embellishment.
  • Importance: Highlight the role of storytelling in communication, cultural transmission, and building connections.

Activity: Group Discussion

Instructions: Discuss the importance of storytelling in various cultures and contexts. Encourage students to share examples of stories they’ve heard growing up.

Elements of a Good Story (20 minutes)

Content:

  • Structure: Introduction, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution.
  • Elements: Setting, characters, plot, conflict, theme, and resolution.
  • Techniques: Descriptive language, dialogue, pacing, and emotional appeal.

Activity: Handout Review

Instructions: Distribute handouts outlining story structure and elements. Review these elements with the class, providing examples and answering questions.

Listening Practice (10 minutes)

Activity: Story Analysis

Instructions: Play a video or audio clip of a well-told story. Ask students to identify the story’s structure and elements.

Follow-Up: Discuss the clip, focusing on how the storyteller uses various techniques to engage the audience.

Story Creation Exercise (25 minutes)

Activity: Story Prompt Writing

Instructions: Provide students with story prompt cards (e.g., “A time you overcame a challenge,” “A surprising event,” “A memorable trip”).

Writing: Students write a short story (1-2 paragraphs) based on the prompt, incorporating the elements and techniques discussed.

Sharing: Students pair up and share their stories with a partner, providing constructive feedback.

Group Activity: Storytelling Performance (15 minutes)

Activity: Storytelling Circle

Instructions: Students form small groups and take turns telling their stories to the group.

Focus: Emphasize the use of expressive language, body language, and vocal variation to enhance the storytelling experience.

Feedback: Group members provide feedback on the storytelling techniques used, offering suggestions for improvement.

Reflection and Homework (5 minutes)

Activity: Class Discussion

Instructions: Reflect on the lesson by discussing what students found challenging and what they enjoyed. Encourage students to share any new storytelling techniques they learned.

Homework:

Assignment: Ask students to refine their written story and practice delivering it. They should record themselves telling the story and submit both the written and recorded versions for review.

Assessment:

  • Participation: Monitor student engagement during activities.
  • Story Creation: Assess students’ written stories for structure, elements, and creativity.
  • Storytelling Performance: Evaluate students’ ability to deliver their stories effectively using narrative techniques.
  • Homework: Review the written and recorded stories for improvement and application of feedback.

Conclusion:

By the end of this lesson, students will have a deeper understanding of storytelling, equipped with practical techniques and vocabulary. They will gain confidence in creating and delivering their own stories, enhancing their communicative competence in English.

Lesson Plan: Small Talk

Lesson Title:

Mastering Small Talk

Target Group:

First-Year Undergraduate ESL Students

Duration:

90 minutes

Lesson Objectives:

  • Students will understand the purpose and importance of small talk in social interactions.
  • Students will learn and practice common small talk topics and phrases.
  • Students will develop confidence in initiating and maintaining small talk in various contexts.

Lesson Outline:

Introduction to Small Talk (10 minutes)

Content:

  • Definition: Explain that small talk is informal conversation used to build rapport and ease into more substantial discussions.
  • Importance: Highlight the role of small talk in social interactions, networking, and cultural integration.

Activity: Group Discussion

Instructions: Discuss with students why small talk is important and share examples of when they have used or encountered small talk in their own lives.

Common Small Talk Topics and Phrases (15 minutes)

Content:

  • Topics: Introduce common small talk topics such as weather, hobbies, current events, travel, and food.
  • Phrases: Provide useful phrases and questions for starting and maintaining small talk.

Activity: Handout Review

Instructions: Distribute handouts with lists of topics and phrases. Review them together, clarifying any unfamiliar vocabulary.

Listening Practice (10 minutes)

Activity: Audio Clips

Instructions: Play audio clips of native speakers engaging in small talk. Ask students to identify the topics and phrases used.

Follow-Up: Discuss the audio clips, focusing on how speakers initiate and maintain the conversation.

Role-Playing Exercises (25 minutes)

Activity: Role-Play Scenarios

Instructions: Divide students into pairs and give each pair a role-play scenario card. Scenarios might include:

  • Meeting a new classmate at the cafeteria.
  • Waiting for the bus with a fellow student.
  • Attending a social event or party.

Practice: Each pair will role-play their scenario focusing on using the small talk phrases and topics discussed.

Feedback: After each role-play, provide feedback on language use, body language, and conversational flow.

Group Activity: Small Talk Circles (10 minutes)

Activity: Rotating Conversations

Instructions: Arrange students in two concentric circles facing each other. Students in the inner circle will rotate every 2 minutes to practice small talk with a new partner from the outer circle.

Topics: Provide a new small talk topic for each rotation.

Reflection and Homework (5 minutes)

Activity: Class Discussion

Instructions: Reflect on the lesson by discussing what students found challenging and what they enjoyed. Encourage students to share their experiences and any new phrases they learned.

Homework:

Assignment: Ask students to engage in a small talk conversation outside of class and write a brief reflection on their experience, focusing on what went well and what they found challenging.

Assessment:

  • Participation: Monitor student engagement during activities.
  • Role-Play Performance: Assess students’ ability to use appropriate phrases and maintain a conversation.
  • Homework: Evaluate the reflection for understanding and application of small talk skills.

Conclusion:

By the end of this lesson, students will have a deeper understanding of small talk, equipped with practical techniques and vocabulary. They will gain confidence in creating and delivering their own stories, enhancing their communicative competence in English.

Lesson Plan: Empathic Listening

Lesson Title

Mastering Empathic Listening

Target Group:

First-Year Undergraduate ESL Students

Duration:

90 minutes

Lesson Objectives:

  • Students will understand the concept and importance of empathic listening.
  • Students will learn and practice techniques for active and empathic listening.
  • Students will develop confidence in applying empathic listening skills in various contexts.

Lesson Outline:

Warm-Up Activity (10 minutes)

Activity: Partner Introduction

Instructions: Pair up students and have them take turns sharing something about themselves (e.g., a hobby or recent experience) while their partner practices listening attentively.

Purpose: To introduce students to the basics of active listening and set the tone for the lesson.

Introduction to Empathic Listening (15 minutes)

Content:

  • Definition: Explain that empathic listening involves fully engaging with the speaker to understand their message, emotions, and perspective.
  • Importance: Highlight the role of empathic listening in building trust, resolving conflicts, and improving communication.

Activity: Group Discussion

Instructions: Discuss the importance of empathic listening in various contexts (e.g., personal relationships, professional settings). Encourage students to share their experiences with both good and poor listening.

Techniques for Empathic Listening (20 minutes)

Content:

  • Active Engagement: Focus on the speaker without distractions.
  • Non-Verbal Cues: Use eye contact, nodding, and appropriate facial expressions to show attentiveness.
  • Verbal Feedback: Provide feedback through paraphrasing, summarizing, and asking clarifying questions.
  • Emotional Resonance: Demonstrate empathy by acknowledging and validating the speaker's feelings.

Activity: Handout Review

Instructions: Distribute handouts outlining empathic listening techniques. Review each technique with examples and answer any questions.

Listening Practice (15 minutes)

Activity: Audio/Video Analysis

Instructions: Play an audio or video clip of a conversation where empathic listening is demonstrated. Ask students to identify the techniques used.

Follow-Up: Discuss the clip, focusing on how the listener effectively used empathic listening skills to engage with the speaker.

Role-Playing Exercises (20 minutes)

Activity: Role-Play Scenarios

Instructions: Divide students into pairs and give each pair a role-play scenario card. Scenarios might include:

  • A friend sharing a personal problem.
  • A colleague discussing a challenging project.
  • A classmate expressing frustration about an assignment.

Practice: Each pair will role-play their scenario with one student practicing empathic listening and the other sharing their story.

Feedback: After each role-play, partners provide feedback on the use of empathic listening techniques.

Group Activity: Empathic Listening Circle (10 minutes)

Activity: Group Sharing

Instructions: Form small groups and have each student share a brief story or concern. Group members practice empathic listening and provide supportive feedback.

Focus: Emphasize the use of verbal and non-verbal cues as well as emotional resonance.

Reflection and Homework (5 minutes)

Activity: Class Discussion

Instructions: Reflect on the lesson by discussing what students found challenging and what they enjoyed. Encourage students to share any new insights they gained about empathic listening.

Homework:

Assignment: Ask students to engage in an empathic listening conversation outside of class (e.g., with a friend or family member) and write a brief reflection on their experience, focusing on the techniques they used and the impact it had on the conversation.

Assessment:

  • Participation: Monitor student engagement during activities.
  • Role-Play Performance: Assess students’ ability to use empathic listening techniques effectively.
  • Group Activity: Evaluate students’ application of empathic listening skills in group discussions.
  • Homework: Review the reflections for understanding and application of empathic listening techniques.

Conclusion:

By the end of this lesson, students will have a deeper understanding of empathic listening and its importance in communication. They will be equipped with practical techniques and the confidence to apply these skills in various contexts, enhancing their communicative competence and fostering better relationships.
